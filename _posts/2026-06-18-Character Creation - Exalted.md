This time character creation takes us to the 3rd Edition of [Exalted](https://theonyxpath.com/category/worlds/exalted/), put out by Onyx Path Publishing. In this game players take on the role of the titular Exalted - normal mortals who are granted divine abilities to act as proxies in a war amongst the gods for control of the Realm. It uses a variant of the Storyteller System from White Wolf's World of Darkness games and like those games, you can expect to find the game's lore and character options spread across a half dozen splatbooks and to throw buckets of d10s for skill checks.

I'll be creating a Solar Exalted (the only type with creation rules included in the core rulebook), the greatest of the Exalted who were the architects of the glorious First Age. After eventually falling from power and being hunted to extinction, people are once again awakening as Solars in a world where they have been vilified for generations by the now-ruling Dragon-Blooded Exalted. Although greatly outnumbered by these Terrestrial Exalted, individual Solars are significantly more powerful.

## Step 1 - Character Concept and Caste
For my Exalted, I'm wanting to lean into the fact that the Solars not only included history's greatest warriors, but also statesmen, artisans and diplomats. As the Chosen of the greatest of the gods, they excel at whatever endeavor they undertake. For my character I picture a Hephaestus type, whose true power is in their ability to create. 

>Tamblin had been fortunate enough to apprentice to an exceptionally talented blacksmith at a young age. He had a natural talent for the craft and his mentor, Sariya, had also shown him all the ins and outs of running the business. She eventually trusted him to run the shop in her absence and on one such occasion he was brought the pieces of a very unique sword by the commander of the local garrison. This Commander Jex wanted to give the reforged weapon as a gift to a visiting dignitary the next day. He let Tamblin know that failure to meet this impossible deadline would be met with severe repercussions. That night as Tamblin worked on the blade as if his life depended on it, he underwent his Exaltation. His anima flared and he found himself seeing patterns of energy flowing through the pieces as if they were alive. He worked through the night in a trance and in the morning the blade was complete with no sign of having been broken. From that day on, Tamblin would use his gift to forge exceptional weapons to help others like him who would be hunted for what they had become.

Most groups of Exalted are broken into castes based on shared role or aptitude. For the Solars, the caste most associated with knowledge and crafting is the Twilight Caste.

At this point, you are told to also make note of the anima effects associated with the caste. Anima is an invisible aura that surrounds all living things which reacts with an Exalted's power to trigger additional effects. The anima of all Solars grants them the ability to always know the exact time and to make their caste mark (a physical symbol on their body denoting their caste) appear at will. The anima effects particular to the Twilight caste are gaining a protective force field, blinking out of existence and appearing elsewhere the following sunset, and bonding a demon or elemental as a familiar.

## Step 2 - Attributes
All attributes start at one dot. They are divided into 3 categories: Physical, Social and Mental, which you designate as your primary, secondary or tertiary category. You receive an additional 8, 6, and 4 dots to distribute among those categories, respectively.

Tamblin's primary category is Mental. After spending 8 dots there he has 2 Perception, 4 Intelligence and 5 Wits.

For the second category I chose Physical because of the demanding work of smithing. Distributing 6 dots give 3 Strength, 2 Dexterity and 4 Stamina.

That leave Mental as his tertiary category with a final spread of 2 Charisma, 3 Manipulation and 2 Appearance.

## Step 3 - Abilities
Five abilities are first chosen from your caste. Since I want to be a crafter the first Twilight ability chosen will be Craft. Craft must be taken with a specialty, so the actual ability will be Craft (Weapons). Eventually I would want Tamblin to be capable of making truly wondrous pieces, which would mean someday purchasing another Craft ability - Craft (Artifacts). That means the next two Twilight abilities to choose must be Lore and Occult, both of which are listed as requirements for starting Superior crafting projects and creating artifacts. A specific focus must be declared when taking Lore, so Tamblin's lore knowledge focuses on the study of anqituities. This leaves two free picks. Of the caste choices available, I will choose Bureaucracy and Linguistics, both of which I feel would be of use to a broker of powerful items.

Next, five Favored abilities are chosen, which may not be the same as previously chosen Caste abilities. I choose Athletics, Integrity, Investigation, Melee and Resistance.

After choosing Caste and Favored abilities, one ability from your Caste abilities is chosen as your Supernal ability, the thing that character is very best at. Mechanically, it is easier to learn Charms with your Supernal ability than any other. For Tamblin, this defining ability is definitely his Craft.

Now comes the part where we get to fill out the character sheet with dots like an old-school Scantron test. 28 dots are given to spend on abilities with the stipulation that no ability can have more than 3 at this point and each Favored ability must start with at least 1. Also, if spending points on non-Caste/non-Favored abilities, this is the time to do it: it's much more expensive to use bonus points later on non-Caste/non-Favored abilities. Starting with 1 each in the Favored abilities and putting the max 3 in crafting related ones (Craft, Lore and Occult), I fill out abilities with an emphasis on the physical as well as skills that would be fitting for someone supplying arms for a budding revolution.

At the end of this step, Tamblin has 2 Athletics, 1 Awareness, 2 Brawl, 1 Bureaucracy, 3 Craft (Weapons), 1 Dodge, 2 Integrity, 2 Investigation, 1 Linguistics, 3 Lore, 3 Melee, 3 Occult, 1 Presence, 2 Resistance and 1 Socialize.

Next, four specialties are chosen. Given his years working the forge, Tamblin is good with a hammer, so Melee (Hammers) make sense, as does Resistance (Overheating). Bureaucracy (Customs) shows his skill at not having his goods held up by official channels. Investigation (Crafts) represents his ability to glean information from close examination of objects made by artisans and crafters.

## Step 4 - Merits
Next are 10 dots to create Merits for the character which are assets or things defining the character other than abilities.

Tamblin takes the following merits:
- Artifact 2 - A 2-dot artifact is a minor wonder that it useful but limited. He has acquired a small orb that produces light on demand that he studies in hopes of making artifacts of his own someday
- Boundless Endurance 2 - This represents the ability to push through exhaustion that would stop others. Gained through his years working the forge.
- Contacts 1 - A 1-dot contacts rating represents contacts at the city or small region level. This reflects his network of suppliers and clients for his smithy
- Mentor 2 - Sariya is his mentor. A 2-dot mentor is an expert in multiple fields. In this case, it represents her teachings in the art of smithing and in business dealings.
- Mighty Thew 2 - This represents exceptional physical strength
- Resources 1 - A level of wealth appropriate for a craftsman or artisan

## Step 5 - Charms
15 Charms are selected which are the descriptors of an Exalted's power. They are tied to your chosen abilities and have minimum requirements. Mechanically, they are used to raise the dice pool when rolling.

The following increase the efficiency of crafting projects:
- Tireless Workhorse Method (min Craft 2)
- Efficient Craftsman Technique (min Craft 3 and Tireless Workhorse Method)

These are used for advanced crafting: (will have to use bonus points in upcoming step to boost these prerequisite stats above 3)
- Arete-Shifting Prana (min Craft 4)
- Supreme Celestial Focus (min Craft 5 and Arete-Shifting Prana)
- Sublime Transference (min Craft 5 and Arete-Shifting Prana)

The following Charms represent using his strength in combat:
- Fists of Iron Technique (min Brawl 1)
- Dipping Swallow Defense (min Melee 1)
- Excellent Strike (min Melee 2)
- Fire and Stones Strike (min Melee 3 and Excellent Strike)

And his physical and mental toughness:
- Enduring Mental Toughness (min Integrity 1)
- Stubborn Boar Defense (min Integrity 2)
- Durability of Oak Meditation (min Resistance 2)
- Spirit Strengthens the Skin (min Resistance 2 and Durability of Oak Meditation)

And a few miscellaneous ones to round out the list:
- Frugal Merchant Method (min Bureaucracy 1) - Can tell quality and condition of goods for sale
- Ancient Tongue Understanding (min Occult 3) - Can temporarily understand ancient languages

## Step 6 - Intimacies and Limit Trigger
Next the character needs at least 4 Intimacies, including at least one Major, one Defining, one positive and one negative. Intimacies are used to represent both bonds the character has with other people in the world as well as principles that they live by.

Tamblin will start with 3 personal relations:
- A major Intimacy with Sariya whom he owes so much of his life to
- A major Initimacy with Commander Jex whom he despises and actively plots against
- A minor Intimacy to the Unconquered Sun, the god who gave him his powers

And 1 defining principle: that he will use his crafting abilities to arm fellow Solars in the resistance against the Dragon-Bloods.

Limit triggers are the events that cause a character to gain Limit - a resource that increases until the character finally hits a breaking point and loses control. For Tamblin, the trigger is when he lets his emotions lead him to a course of action that he later regrets.

## Step 7 - Bonus Points
15 bonus points are given to spend on any final changes needed to complete the character to match your vision. Costs vary, but these points can be used on pretty much anything: to raise attributes, gain additional abilities or push abilities above 3 dots, or to gain more specialties, Merits, Charms or Willpower.

Tamblin needs to use some of these points to boost ablity scores above 3 in order to meet the Craft requirement of some of the Charms he took above. Luckily, it is extremely cheap to spend bonus points on Caste or Favored abilities - only 1 point per dot - so he will raise his Craft (Weapons) all the way to 5, as well as Occult and Lore to 4 each, for a total of 4 points. These levels are also needed to start significant crafting projects down the road.

Next he'll boost a few of his secondary attributes - Strength and Dexterity - by a dot each. It's 3 points to boost a dot of tertiary attribute but 4 per dot of primary or secondary, so this takes another 8 points.

He'll raise a dot in Melee for another point (only 1 since it's a Favored ability) and with his remaining 2 points add another dot of Willpower.

## Step 8 - Finishing Touches
Mark Essence-related stats on the character sheet.
- Start with an Essence rating of 1.
- Calculate personal Essence pool = (Essence x 3) + 10 = 13
- Calculate peripheral Essence pool = (Essence x 7) + 26 = 33

Unless something in character creation indicates otherwise, take the default Health levels:
-0, -1, -1, -2, -2, -4, Incapacitated

Start with a Willpower of 5. Since Tamblin used bonus points on an extra Willpower dot, he will instead start with 6.

## Step 9 - Even More Finishing Touches
While not listed in the character creation checklist, there are still some more items to finish filling out on a character sheet.

Characters can start with any reasonable gear, so Tamblin will have his hammer and a buff jacket.
- Under the weapons section I will add the hammer which is a medium weapon, so a damage of +9. Set Withering as Dexterity + Melee + Accuracy (2 for the hammer) so 3 + 4 + 2 = 9. Decisive is the same without the Accuracy, so 7.

- Under the Health & Defense section, fill in Natural Soak equal to Stamina (4), Armor Soak if wearing armor (+3 for the buff jacket), and Total Soak as the sum of the two.

- For Hardness, write any hardness granted by Charms or magical armor. Tamblin gets 4 points of Hardness from his Durability of Oak Meditation.

- Parrying with the hammer will be ((Dexterity + Melee) / 2) + the hammer's defense = ((3 + 4) / 2) + 1 = 5

- Resolve = (Wits + Integrity + specialty) / 2, rounded up = (5 + 2 + 0) / 2 = 4

- Guile = (Manipulation + Socialize + specialty) / 2, rounded up = (3 + 1 + 0) / 2 = 2

- Join Battle = Wits + Awareness. 5 + 1 = 6

- Evasion = ((Dexterity + Dodge) / 2, rounded up) - armor mobility penalty = ((3 + 1) / 2) - 0 = 2

- Rush Pool = Dexterity + Athletics = 3 + 2 = 5

- Disengage Pool = Dexterity + Dodge = 3 + 1 = 4

## Final Thoughts
This system is ... a lot. And this was just creating the character, not trying to run it in play. I like that the system really does give you enough tools to pretty much create whatever kind of overpowered demigod that you want. But it's a huge book and you do have to go back and forth through a lot of it to get all your Merits and Charms and the math for everything sorted. And this is just for creating Solar Exalted. Other types require completely different books and follow different procedures. You really need to hope everyone's onboard for a long campaign with this one as it seems it would take so much work upfront to bring everyone's character concepts to the table.
