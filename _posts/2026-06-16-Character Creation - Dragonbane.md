For my first character creation writeup, I'll be going with Free League's [Dragonbane](https://freeleaguepublishing.com/games/dragonbane/), a NuSR game featuring great artwork, skill-based progression, a d20 roll-under mechanic, and most controversially to some people, anthropomorphic ducks as a playable race.

Character creation in Dragonbane is a fairly straightforward affair, following a checklist with options at each step to either roll on provided tables or provide your own values. I do love me some good old-fashioned roll tables so I'll be going with that option for the most part.

## Step 1: Choose or roll your kin
Kin is Dragonbane's term for race/species, and the default options are Human, Halfling, Dwarf and Elf, along with Mallard and Wolfkin, both of which are pretty self-explanatory. I know I said I'd let the fates guide this character but I'm already going to break that rule on the first step. My character is 100% going to be a Mallard, because when else am I going to get that as a base choice in a system.

## Step 2: Note your innate ability
Each kin comes with its own innate ability that no other kin has access to. For Mallards, two innate abilities are given, for a real Donald Duck vibe: Ill-Tempered and Webbed Feet. Ill-Tempered gives you the option to take a boon (advantage) on a skill roll in exchange for taking the Angry condition. Being Angry gives a bane (disadvantage) on all rolls for INT and INT-based skills. Webbed Feet gives a boon to all swimming rolls and negates the normal speed penalty when in or under water.

## Step 3: Choose or roll your profession
Profession is what you did before you were an adventurer, but the choice is more meaningful than the random profession in many OSR games. Your profession provides a list of starting skills to choose from, as well as gear and your first heroic ability, but further advancement isn't tied to your profession in the same way as it would be in a strictly class-based system. There are some professions that stick very close to traditional classes in other games, such as Figher, Mage and Thief, but Dragonbane also includes options such as Artisan, Mariner and Merchant.

Rolling on the d10 Profession table I get a 9, which gives my irritable duck-man the profession of Scholar.

## Step 4: Choose or roll your age
Next up is age, which does have mechanical implications. Characters are divided broadly into young, adult or old. The actual age in years is irrelevant and assumed to be whatever would be evbiate for a given kin. Characters have worse stats if they are in the higher age categories, but this is balanced by their extra life experience which gives them more skills to choose at character creation.

Rolling a 3 on a d6 places my scholar in the young category. This provides a +1 bonus to agility and constitution, but means they only get to pick 2 additional trained skills past the 6 already chosen from the profession list, as opposed to members of the adult and old categories who would have 4 or 6, respectively.

## Step 5: Choose or roll your name
Each kin provides a small sample of names. The book also suggests you might want a nickname for your adventurer, with a small table of nicknames given for each profession. Rolling a d6 on each table gives me a 1 for name and 5 for nickname, providing a name of Qwucksum from the Mallard list, and a nickname of "The All-Knowing" from the scholar list. Qwucksum the All-Knowing is a truly cursed and terrible name, so of course I like it. I imagine the all-knowing moniker was given as an ironic insult by their peers but Qwucksum is too self-absorbed to notice the intent and bears it proudly.

## Step 6: Roll your attributes
Dragonbane takes a middle ground in assigning stats. It recommends a 4d6, drop lowest system, with stats assigned as they are rolled (to any open stat, not necessarily in order), and once finished two scores may be swapped. So while not as open-ended as a system that lets you assign stats after seeing all 6 values, it's certainly not as punishing as 3d6 in order.

Bearing in mind that I need my Intelligence as the high stat for a scholar, here we go:

- Value 1: 6, 1, ~~1~~, 3 = 10. A nice, average stat. I'll give that to Willpower, which determine Willpower Points.
- Value 2: 5, ~~2~~, 4, 3 = 12. Decent, not great. I'll give that to Agility.
- Value 3: 2, 3, 4, ~~2~~ = 9. I kind of expect Strength to be a dump stat, but still need to be able to carry my gear so I wouldn't want to go any lower than this.
- Value 4: 4, 4, 5, ~~1~~ = 13. Starting to run out of stats here and still don't have a great one, so I'll put this in Intelligence.
- Value 5: ~~1~~, 6, 6, 5 = 17. D'oh, assigned my INT too soon. I'll put this in Constitution with the assumption that I'll be swapping it with INT which will give a nice value for my key attribute and decent HP to boot.
- Value 6: 3, 2, ~~1~~, 2 = 7. Ouch. My one stat left is Charisma, which is fine. I don't imagine someone with a starting ability of Ill-Tempered is likeable in the slightest.

Swapping CON and INT and adding in the +1 AGL and CON from having an age of Young, the final array is:

| STR | CON | AGL | INT | WIL | CHA |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 9   | 14  | 13  | 17  | 10  | 7   |

## Step 7. Calculate your derived ratings.
The first derived rating is movement speed (in meters). Qwucksum has a base of 8 for being a Mallard, then a +2 for having 13 AGL, for a total of 10.

Damage bonuses are next. 9 STR is not great so no bonus there, but at least there's not a penalty. 13 AGL, on the other hand, is good enough for a +d4 bonus when using agility-based weapons.

HP is equal to CON, giving a respectable 14 for Qwucksum.

Finally, WP is equal to WIL, so 10.

## Step 8. Choose your trained skills.
First select six skills from the ones provided by your profession. I imagine my young scholar as the Indiana Jones type, out getting their hands dirty in old temples in the pursuit of knowledge, so I'll pick the skills from the scholar list accordingly: Awareness, Evade, Healing, Languages, Myths & Legends and Spot Hidden.

The remaining trained skills can be chosen at will. As a Young adventurer I only get to select 2 additional skills. I'll take Bushcraft and since I should probably take an agility-based weapon skill, Knives.

All skills in Dragonbane are associated with a certain attribute and the character's base skill depends on their attribute score. Trained skills have a skill level equal to twice the base. All of Qwucksum's trained skills (other than Evade and Knives) are tied to INT. Their INT of 17 gives a base chance of 7 and thus a final skill level of 14. The remaining two skills are AGL-based. With an AGL of 13, the base is 6 and the trained level is 12.

## Step 9. Note your heroic ability.
In Dragonbane you can get more heroic abilities through play, most commonly by maxing a stat out at 18, but each adventurer gets one to start which is determined by their profession. For my scholar the starting heroic ability is Intuition, the ability to spend willpower points to ask the GM directly for a helpful answer about a difficult decision.

## Step 10. Choose or roll your weakness (optional).
Weaknesses are an optional feature that can be attached to a character. If a character has a weakness and then the player roleplays this weakness during a session, they make take an extra advancement mark at end of session. If they roleplay to show how they overcome this weakness, they take another advancement mark (for a total of 2 instead of 1) and clear the weakness. After a subsequent session of playing without a weakness, the player may take another weakness as established in the fiction.

A d20 table of suggestions for weaknesses is provided. I roll a 9 which gives "Gluttonous - I take every chance I get to eat something tasty." I imagine Qwucksum spends so much time out tomb-raiding that they really go to town when back in civilization.

## Step 11. Choose or roll your gear.
Each profession comes with 3 different kits of starting gear. Rolling a d6 for gear from the scholar list I get a 6, which gives me a short sword, bandages, sleeping poison, a sleeping pelt, lantern, lamp oil, flint & tinder, d6 (6) food rations and d10 (6) silver. 
With a short sword and poison, and no type of book or notebook like the other scholar starting kits, this definitely seems to fit with the Indiana Jones vibe.

Qwucksum's low encumbrance is a bit of a problem here. Every 4 rations count as an inventory slot and they're already maxed out, so they'll have to toss out 2 of the rations to not waste an extra slot. I guess the gluttonous weakness is perfect in this case - Qwucksum simply ate 2 of their starting rations before even setting out.

I think at this point I'd also use 5 of the 6 silver to buy a knife to use as a weapon instead of the short sword to be able to leverage the weapon training. The bonus damage for using an agility-based weapon with Qwucksum's stats more than makes up for stepping down from the short sword's d10 to the knife's d8.

## Step 12. Choose or roll your memento (optional).
Mementos are an optional rule that allow your character to carry a small item that has no practical use but great sentimental value. Once per session during a stretch rest (Dragonbane's terminology for a short rest, compared to the longer shift rest) you can use your memento to clear an additional condition.

A d20 table is provided with suggestions. Rolling 5 gives "a bracelet passed down in your family." I think this bracelet is clearly old, possibly ancient, and has been passed down for several generations in the family although nobody knows its origin. It bears engravings in an unknown script that young Qwucksum has spent countless hours examining over the years, and their determination to find out its origin is what led them to become a scholar.

## Step 13. Choose or roll your appearance.
The book suggests including one or two distinctive features for the character. A table with suggestions is, of course, provided.

A few rolls on a d20 gives 4 - "a constant smile on your lips" and 20 - "weathered face." I think Qwucksum is way too confident in their own abilities and has a constant smug grin on their face. The mental image is a bit disconcerting since Qwucksum has a duck bill instead of actual lips. I'm not sure how a duck's face looks weathered. Maybe Qwucksum is missing a few feathers from his face despite their young age.

## Conclusion
Qwucksum is now ready for action. They're a smug, young adventurer with a thirst for knowledge and some reasonably good skills to back that up. While they won't be winning any contests for personality or arm-wrestling, they're competent at the skills needed to help a party make its way through the wilderness and in a forgotten dungeon, able to take a hit, and not terrible in a fight if it comes down to it. Just don't get between them and their food.
